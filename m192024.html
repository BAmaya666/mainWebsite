Cherry Bumm

May19, 2024

Link to Cherry Bumm on Figma

The Story

By the beginning of 2024 I decided to get started on coding and UX, but I hadn’t had any idea of which project I could develop. A game maybe?

It was until one morning in March, when my boyfriend and I were walking around the neighborhood looking at the plants in the gardens. So many! Some started to show fruits and berries, some smelled delicious, but I couldn’t name any.

I was already aware of the rich diversity of plants in British Columbia since the day I moved here, and I got a book, “Edible and Medicinal Plants of Canada”, to carry around. But the reality is that it’s a chunky book that adds a lot of weight to my bag, so it’s lying beautifully in the bookshelf at home.

Anyway, I took some pictures of the plants to identify them with Google Lens, but the experience was slow, vague, and disappointing.

That’s when everything clicked, and I saw this situation as an opportunity to tackle it with design ✨.

I just started to do sketches on paper, how I like it to behave and what it should do. So I looked into the book, how it’s structured, and see if I can standardize its content for the app.

It felt like the app was building itself; everything flowed naturally:

You just want to eliminate friction in your experience identifying a plant, so I thought of Shazam (the music identifier app), and once you know its name and what it does, maybe you want to save that info in your profile and maybe pin it (like in Google Maps), so people also interested in this topic can see the plant in the app map. Maybe they want to meet people with the same interests and create a community (like Discord) and maybe even meet in real life (MeetUp).

So after gathering all the ideas for my app, I made up a user journey and started designing the app on Figma. Finally finish my first project on Figma by the end of March.

The Name

When I was thinking of a name, I wanted to give it a name that sounds like a term of endearment but with fruits, like “sweet pea” or “pumpkin pie.”

Lost in translation, I thought cherry bum was also a common nickname (it was honey bun haha), and my boyfriend mentioned that that’s the name of the vegan restaurant in Commercial Drive (My Sweet Cherubim) that we go to often, but we can never say its name correctly. We laugh, and I decided to name it Cherry Bum as it links the app with Vancouver East Side more.

A friend even thought of fireworks (cherry bomb) when I told him about it. That gave me ideas for the mascot: a fruit with an explosive but sweet personality.

What does it do

Essentially, you take a picture of the plant, berry, or bush you want to identify with the app, and it tells you its name and the description (if it’s edible, if the native people used it for such and such, if it’s poisonous, its medicinal uses, etc.).

If you want to go beyond, you can sign up and help the community to grow its data: you can see a map with all the pins other users left, events happening near you, forums, and your profile with a list of the pins you pinned, the events you are going to and your direct messages (oh yeah!) with fellow plant enthusiasts.

It stopped there until September when I started to update my professional portfolio, and for this time I knew that to get my feet wet in the industry I have to make it on my own website and that I have to show my thought process more emphatically (I will talk more profoundly about it in my next post), and for now I published it on Framer, which is a tool to make responsive websites without coding, and also Figma has a plug-in to move your prototype to Framer and make it responsive.

Please check my Cherry Bum project on my portfolio here. It has the actual research and design thinking.
